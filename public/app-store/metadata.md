## Full Description (App Store — 4000 chars max)

**Chat in any language. Be understood by everyone.**

Evrywher is the AI-powered translation messenger built for a world without language barriers. Whether you're connecting with Japanese business partners, chatting with Arabic-speaking clients, or messaging friends in Korean, Portuguese, or Hindi — Evrywher translates every message instantly, in real-time.

**You type in your language. They read in theirs. Magic.**

---

### ✨ What makes Evrywher different

**Real-time AI Translation**
Every message — text, voice, or image — is automatically translated by advanced AI. No copy-pasting into Google Translate. No delays. Just natural conversation.

**21 Languages Supported**
English, Japanese, Arabic, Korean, Chinese, Hindi, Portuguese, Spanish, French, Urdu, Bengali, Turkish, Filipino, Vietnamese, Persian, Pashto, Thai, Indonesian, Nepali, Sinhala, Dutch — and growing.

**Cultural Intelligence**
Evrywher doesn't just translate words — it explains cultural context. Understand the nuances behind "お疲れ様" or "Inshallah" with instant cultural notes that appear alongside translations.

**Voice Messages, Translated**
Record a voice message in English. Your contact hears it in Japanese. Evrywher transcribes your voice, translates it, and even generates a translated audio version in their language.

**Group Chats Across Languages**
Add contacts who speak different languages to the same group. Every member reads every message in their own language — automatically. No one is left out.

**AI Personal Assistant**
Your built-in AI bot handles the conversations you're too busy for. Set it up with your personality, business rules, and language — it auto-replies and translates while you focus on what matters.

**QR Code Contact Sharing**
Share your Evrywher profile via QR code. One scan and anyone can start a translated conversation with you — no accounts required on their side.

**Works for Everyone**
- Freelancers working with international clients
- Business owners in multilingual markets
- Expats living abroad
- Travelers navigating unfamiliar countries
- Families separated by language
- Teachers and students across cultures
